Safety

Key Concepts and Vocabulary

Privacy and Data Protection

  • Cookies: Small data files stored on your device by websites to remember preferences and track behavior.
  • Dark Patterns: Deceptive UX techniques that manipulate users into making unintended choices.
  • Privacy Settings: Controls that allow users to manage their personal information on digital platforms.
  • Terms of Service: Legal agreement between a service provider and the user, outlining usage rules.
  • GDPR: EU law on data protection and privacy, influential globally.

Digital Media and Brain Impact

  • Addictive Design: Features in digital products that encourage compulsive use.
  • Humane Design: Creating digital products that respect users' time, attention, and wellbeing.
  • Digital Detox: Period of time when a person refrains from using electronic devices.

Online Relationships and Communication

  • Sexting: Sending sexually explicit messages or images electronically.
  • Self-disclosure: Sharing personal information or feelings with others online.
  • Red-flag feeling: Uncomfortable emotion triggered by online interactions.

Cyberbullying and Hate Speech

  • Hate Speech: Communication that attacks a person based on attributes like race, religion, or sexual orientation.
  • Cyberbullying: Using digital platforms to intimidate, harm, or upset someone.
  • Doxing: Publishing private or identifying information about an individual on the internet.

Digital Impersonation and Identity Theft

  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone else online.
  • Identity Theft: Stealing personal information for fraudulent purposes.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.
  • Deep Fake: Synthetic media where a person's likeness is replaced with someone else's.

Critical Advice for Digital Safety

  1. Regularly review and update privacy settings on all platforms.
  2. Use complex, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  3. Think critically before sharing personal information or content online.
  4. Be aware of addictive design tactics and set healthy usage boundaries.
  5. Report and stand against cyberbullying and hate speech.
  6. Verify the authenticity of online profiles and be cautious of impersonation.
  7. Educate yourself on the latest cybersecurity threats and protection methods.
  8. Regularly monitor your digital footprint and online reputation.
  9. Practice empathy and respect in all online interactions.
  10. Seek help from trusted adults or authorities if facing online threats or harassment.
